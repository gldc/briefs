## Key stories

**Semble: Code search for agents that uses 98% fewer tokens than grep**
A new search tool called Semble has been developed specifically for AI agents to navigate codebases. By drastically reducing token consumption compared to traditional grep, it addresses a major bottleneck in agentic workflows, paving the way for faster and more cost-effective autonomous coding.
Source: https://github.com/MinishLab/semble

**Multiple commencement speakers booed for AI comments during graduation speeches**
Audiences at recent graduation ceremonies have audibly rejected speakers who praised or heavily referenced artificial intelligence. This backlash highlights a growing public skepticism and fatigue toward AI hype when inserted into non-technical, human-centric contexts.
Source: https://www.nbcnews.com/video/multiple-commencement-speakers-booed-for-ai-comments-during-graduation-speeches-263486021518

**Students unveil affordable hearing aids**
A student-led project has introduced a low-cost alternative to expensive hearing aid technology. This development showcases the continued democratization of health tech, making critical medical assistance more accessible to broader populations.
Source: Superhuman AI Newsletter

**ChatGPT offers personal finance advice**
ChatGPT is increasingly being leveraged for personal financial planning and data analysis in Excel. The trend indicates AI is expanding its utility from generalized assistance to specialized, structured financial tasks, potentially disrupting traditional advisory services.
Source: Superhuman AI Newsletter
